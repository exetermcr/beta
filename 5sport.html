In College, well you must try rowing at least once in your life and we have a really nice boathouse in the middle of boathouse Island http://www.ourcs.org.uk/clubs/exet. Best Term to start rowing? Michaelmas Term also known as “Novice Term” in boatie jargon. I must say also coxing it is a really good fun a must try. If you want to give a go to something really special, try Real Tennis. Exeter is the only college to have its own real tennis club and our players are fortunate to benefit from subsidies, so you can start playing very cheaply. There is no ‘all-whites’ clothing rule and you can borrow a racquet. It is probably the most difficult racquet sport and the rules are complex. For more information, contact the ECRTC (Exeter College Real Tennis Club) Captain, Maggie Henderson-Tew by e-mailing her at maggie.henderson-tew@exeter.ox.ac.uk.

Badminton

Exeter College Badminton Club meets 4-6pm at Magdalen College School every Saturday to train and play games. This is a relaxed and friendly session for any Exonian of any ability; we have a lot of available court space, rackets that can be borrowed and regular players who can give tips if required. Throughout the year we compete in inter-college leagues in changing teams of either 4 men, 4 women or a mixed team of 2 men and 2 women all playing both singles and doubles matches and we're always looking for new members. If you want to play competitively at the college level or just have a good knock-about please come along and get involved! For more info contact Exeter College Badminton Club President Adam Ward at adam.ward@exeter.ox.ac.uk

Exeter College punt scheme

The booking of punts starts on Sunday 27th April 2014 in Trinity Term, and finishes the end of 9th week. Bookings can only be made 24 hours in advance e.g. 10.00 am Monday for 10.00 am Tuesday on the hour. Bookings can only be made by the person booking the Punt and not by his/her friends. The Porter will note whether the student booking is from the JCR or the MCR; both common rooms will use the same punt. Porters must put the name of the person booking the punt on the back of the ticket and stamp the ticket with the Exeter College stamp. When collecting a punt, students must produce a punt ticket together with a University or Bodleian Card. If you unable to produce these items then NO PUNT. Only one booking per person per day.
